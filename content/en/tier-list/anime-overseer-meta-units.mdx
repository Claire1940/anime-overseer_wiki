<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Evolution and Progression Guide">

Evolution is the most significant power spike a unit can receive in Anime Overseer. It doesn't just increase base stats; it often unlocks new abilities or increases attack range, which is vital for handling tougher enemy waves. However, evolution is expensive and requires specific materials that are often limited to code rewards or high-level farming.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish a Core Team">
    Before evolving anything, ensure you have a full lineup of usable units. A single evolved unit cannot carry a team of level 1 placeholders.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Max Upgrades First">
    Use Gold and Coins to upgrade your units to their current level cap. Evolution provides the best results when applied to a unit that is already performing well.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gather Materials">
    Collect Soul Crystals, Super Soul Crystals, and specific food items like Meat or Onigiri. Check active codes like `rerollcode` and `THX4500` for these materials.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Evolution">
    Only evolve units that fit into your long-term team plan. Prioritize units that help with co-op runs or endless-style progression.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Scarcity">
Super Soul Crystals are rare. Do not spend them on C-Tier units or characters you plan to replace within a week. Save them for your S-Tier carries to ensure you don't hit a progression wall.
</Callout>

| Material | Primary Source | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Soul Crystals** | Codes, Boss Drops | Standard unit evolution. |
| **Super Soul Crystals** | Milestone Codes | High-tier/Legendary evolution. |
| **Relic Shards** | Endless Mode | Advanced progression and Relic builds. |
| **Food (Meat/Onigiri)** | Codes, Daily Rewards | General unit growth and level boosting. |

Testing your evolved units in real battle scenarios is the final step. Compare their clear speed and survival rates against their pre-evolution performance. If the improvement is marginal, you may need to adjust your placement strategy or look for better Traits.

</Section>
