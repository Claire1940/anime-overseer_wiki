<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Your First Match">

Entering your first battle can be overwhelming if you do not know where to place your units or how to manage the wave economy. Follow these steps to secure your first victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Deploy Your Starter Units">
    Place your primary damage dealers near corners or intersections where their attack range covers the maximum amount of the path. Avoid placing all units in a single cluster at the start of the map.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage the Mid-Wave Economy">
    As you defeat enemies, you will earn Gold and battle currency. Spend this currency to upgrade your placed units rather than placing new, unupgraded units. Upgraded units gain massive stat multipliers that are essential for clearing boss targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Analyze Enemy Weaknesses">
    Watch the pathing of different enemy types. If fast-moving runners are leaking past your defense, place a support unit with slowing abilities or adjust your target settings to focus on the fastest enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collect Post-Match Rewards">
    Winning or losing a match rewards you with account experience, Gems, Gold, and potential evolution materials. Return to the lobby to upgrade your characters before starting the next map.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Co-Op Advantage">

</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Resource Management & Trait Rerolls">

Resources are limited, and wasting them early is the most common mistake new players make during Early Access. Understanding which materials to spend and which to save will keep your account efficient.

| Resource | Best Early-Game Use | Spending Priority | Common Waste to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gems** | Summoning units to fill out your starting roster | **High** | Chasing a single unit before securing a balanced team |
| **Gold** | Upgrading the specific units that fix your current wave walls | **High** | Leveling up temporary bench units that you will replace |
| **Trait Rerolls** | Improving traits on proven, long-term carry units | **Save** | Rerolling traits on starter or filler units |
| **Soul Crystals** | Saving for unit evolution requirements | **Save** | Spending on early-game progression before choosing a main team |

<Callout type="warning" title="Trait Reroll Warning">
Trait Discs and Trait Rerolls are highly valuable. Only use them on units that are guaranteed to stay in your active lineup for a long time. Rerolling a temporary unit's traits wastes resources that are difficult to farm later.
</Callout>
