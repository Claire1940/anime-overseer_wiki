To build an unstoppable roster in Anime Overseer, you must follow a structured upgrade path. Spreading your resources too thin among multiple low-tier units is the most common mistake new players make during Early Access.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Redeem Active Codes">

  <Step num="2" title="Assemble a Balanced Lineup">
    Summon your first batch of units. Aim for a mix of cheap single-target damage dealers to handle early wave runners and AoE (Area of Effect) units for crowd control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear Wave Milestones">
    Enter standard battle modes. Focus on learning enemy paths, wave timings, and identifying which of your units are underperforming during late-stage rushes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade Before Rerolling">
    Use your Gold to upgrade the levels of your primary carry units. Level upgrades provide a guaranteed power spike compared to the random nature of trait rerolling.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Prepare for Evolution">
    Once you identify your absolute best units, stockpile Soul Crystals and Relic Shards. Evolving a unit completely transforms its skill set and damage ceiling.
  </Step>

| Resource Type | Confirmed Code Sources | Primary Function | Ideal Target |

| **Trait Rerolls** | wunbosavedtheday! | Changes unit's passive trait | High-tier S-Tier carries |
| **Disc Rerolls** | AXISTHEGOAT | Alternative trait adjustment | Evolved units only |
| **Super Soul Crystals** | rerollcode, THX4500 | Advanced evolution material | Top-tier meta fighters |

Only invest your Trait Discs and Rerolls into units that are already evolved or are guaranteed to stay in your active lineup for a long time. Rerolling traits on a temporary B-Tier starter unit is a massive waste of progression speed.

<Callout type="info" title="Reroll Priority">
If you pull a top-tier damage dealer, prioritize rolling for traits that boost Damage percentage, Attack Speed, or Range. Support units benefit most from Range and Cooldown Reduction.
